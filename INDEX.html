HISTORY OF DUCATI

Ducati (company)

A month after the official liberation of Italy in 1944, SIATA announced its intention to sell this engine, called the "Cucciolo" (Italian for "puppy," in reference to the distinctive exhaust sound) to the public.

The first Cucciolos were available alone, to be mounted on standard bicycles, by the buyer; however, businessmen soon bought the little engines in quantity, and offered complete motorized-bicycle units for sale. In 1950, after more than 200,000 Cucciolos had been sold, in collaboration with SIATA, the Ducati firm offered its own Cucciolo-based motorcycle.

SCRAMBLE SERIES

Ducati Scrambler: Aimed at more casual riding experience, Scrambler series includes models like Scrambler Icon and Scrambler 1100, which are designed for versatility and style, appealing to both new and experienced riders.The Scrambler's 803cc models generally offer a 6-speed gearbox, making them versatile for city commuting and light off-road, while 1100 models provide more power for longer trips.

SPORTS BIKES

Ducati Panigale Series: Known for their high performance and racing capabilities, the Panigale models, such as the Panigale V4 and Panigale V2, feature powerful engines and advanced aerodynamics, making them ideal for track enthusiasts. Ducati SuperSport: This model combines sportbike performance with everyday usability, offering a comfortable riding position and versatile capabilities for both track and street riding.

ADVENTURE AND TOURING BIKES

Ducati Multistrada: Designed for versatility, the Multistrada series, including the Multistrada V4, is equipped for both on-road and off-road adventures, featuring advanced technology for safety and comfort. Ducati DesertX: A dual-purpose motorcycle that excels in off-road conditions while being capable of long-distance touring, featuring a robust chassis and powerful engine.
